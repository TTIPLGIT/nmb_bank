    Cloud reduces IT spend by 22 percent, boosts secure data access for a Kenyan audit office

    Print

    May 11, 2020

    The Office of the Auditor General-Kenya (OAG) is an independent, public-sector agency mandated to audit the accounts of national and county governments as well as their subsidiaries. The organization aimed to facilitate document management and ensure maximum uptime of critical apps, but it was struggling with limited IT resources. By moving its on-premises infrastructure, networking, and storage to Microsoft Azure, OAG reduced IT administration costs by 22 percent, while reaching up to 99.99 percent application uptime.

    The Office of the Auditor General-Kenya (OAG) is an independent organization responsible for auditing the performance of the Kenyan government. “Put simply, the job of OAG is to ensure the public that money is being spent properly by the authorities,” says Christopher Lilako, Software and Applications Team Leader at OAG. The organization’s value and reputation rest upon trust in the reliability and consistency of its audits. To warrant security and transparency in audit execution, OAG migrated applications, databases, storage, identity, network, and compute power to Azure IaaS and PaaS solutions. The implementation was entirely run by Next Technologies, a Kenyan software integrator that had supported OAG’s digital transformation projects in the past.

    Increased workload

    “We decided that we were going to conduct our work using technology. For example, instead of having paper files, we would use software to document our findings and publish reports, whereby data should be submitted online,” says Domenic Mutiria Kamenyi, Director ICT at OAG. In the beginning, the organization was hosting essential software and applications in its own datacenter. However, the rigid on-premises infrastructure lacked resiliency and agility to support the increasing scalability demands. “At some point, we started running into problems scaling up and down our infrastructure,” Lilako explains. “Scaling up was especially difficult because we had to install new equipment, and the procurement process is very long. We felt it would be better if we had some workloads running in the cloud to be able to scale up and scale down easily at any point,” he adds. 

    Initially, OAG was considering the deployment of a second on-premises datacenter to simplify and streamline the management of its applications. However, after evaluating the costs and efforts involved, the organization leaned towards the cloud. “OAG wanted to upgrade their datacenter but discovered it was actually cheaper to move to the cloud,” explains Amos Wanjie, Microsoft Pre-Sales Lead at Next Technologies. Since OAG had already been taking advantage of Microsoft cloud apps, management decided to go with Azure. According to Lilako, “We had already been using Office 365 for around two years, so we felt it would be easier to integrate Azure than any other cloud service provider into the environment, and would save us a lot of reconfigurations.”

    99.9 percent application uptime, less IT work

    By migrating to the cloud, OAG unlocked immediate IT savings and gained the capabilities to develop, run, and manage mission-critical business apps without having to build and maintain its own infrastructure. “When we share things, the cost goes down,” Kamenyi explains. The move to the cloud significantly reduced workload for the organization’s IT department, allowing team members to shift focus from menial install-manage-upgrade tasks to more pertinent project work. Altogether, through automation, dynamic scalability, and a cost-effective consumption model, the implemented IaaS and PaaS solutions have reduced OAG’s IT administration costs by 22 percent. 

    Cost efficiency isn’t the only measurable benefit. The new cloud infrastructure helped the organization resolve the challenge of application availability. “Applications are optimal when you go to Azure; you cannot compare with it when you are running systems on-premises,” Kamenyi notes. With Azure, OAG is guaranteed an uptime of up to 99.9 percent to avoid performance issues and downtime. “Before we went to the cloud, the uptime was around 60 percent in a month. On cloud services, it’s more than 95 percent,” says Kamenyi. Lilako agrees. “The environment in the cloud is always stable,” he adds.

    Security-rich information access

    As a public auditor, OAG handles large volumes of private and confidential information that must be duly protected and processed in a secure, compliant way. The Azure cloud platform provides an entirely safe environment for storing and accessing sensitive audit data. “You cannot afford to lose some information, even for a split second,” points out Kamenyi.

    OAG has several auditors seated in the country, working remotely. In the past, they stored sensitive data on their laptops, which gave OAG little control over where the information would reside. Today, the organization can record and update all data centrally on Azure―without additional backup. It offers tamper-proof storage enhanced by Microsoft investments in cloud security and compliance. “Azure security gives us an overview of all the applications that are running and their status in terms of vulnerabilities,” explains Lilako. “Our data is being backed up using Azure and dependent systems,” adds Kamenyi.

    A new era of technology-driven audit  

    Always open to digital innovation, OAG is continuously exploring the capabilities of Azure services to improve daily audit practices. According to Lilako, “One of the ways we communicate to the public is through an audit report, which we publish on our website. We have a new website running on Azure, with a much friendlier user interface than the old one. And there, the public can access these reports quickly or search for specific topics within the website. Previously, they couldn’t do that.”

    Currently, OAG is working with Microsoft and Next Technologies on several projects. The organization is deploying a Windows Virtual Desktop (VDI) solution to boost the productivity of field auditors and support top executives traveling around the country with remote and secure access to data. OAG is also looking to enhance its reporting data collection and analytics, implement Azure services for better internal and external communication, and apply Azure Cognitive Services for AI-powered, automated audits.

    “Our IT department appreciates the use of the cloud. They can see the difference in time when they need to scale up and down, and in terms of availability,” concludes Lilako.
